About me

My name is Turki Bin Hammad. I am an epidemiologist interested in causal inference using both randomized controlled trials and observational data to estimate the causal effects of treatments and vaccines. I have a background in pharmacy and more than 4 years of experience as a regulatory reviewer of the efficacy and safety of human treatments and vaccines. I recently graduated with a master degree in epidemiology with distinction and did my project on utilizing routinely collected health data for inferences about under-represented populations in randomized trials using target trial framework. My interest started with my first review. It was a review of a single arm controlled trial for late stage cancer drug. This was the most challenging task to do. To try and find causal effects from single arm studies. Since then, I believed that finding real causal effects from pharmacological intervention will impact the whole patient population.
